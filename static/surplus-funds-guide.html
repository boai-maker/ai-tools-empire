What Are Surplus Funds?

When a property is sold at a tax sale or foreclosure auction, sometimes the sale price exceeds what was owed in back taxes or liens. That extra money — the "surplus" — belongs to the former property owner.

But here's the problem: counties don't contact you about it. The money just sits there, unclaimed, until someone files a claim or the statute of limitations expires.

Who Might Be Owed Money?

  • Anyone who lost a property to a tax sale
  • Heirs of deceased property owners
  • Former owners of foreclosed properties
  • People who moved and never received notice

How Much Money Are We Talking About?

Individual claims range from a few hundred dollars to over $50,000. The average claim we process is $8,000-$15,000.

How to Check If You're Owed Money

Every county maintains a list of unclaimed surplus funds. You can check your county's tax collector or clerk of court website. Search for "surplus funds" or "excess proceeds" along with your county name.
