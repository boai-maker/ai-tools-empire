1. Business Plans & Strategy (10 Prompts)

#1
Investor-Ready Business Plan
Write a comprehensive business plan for [business idea]. Include: executive summary, market analysis with TAM/SAM/SOM, competitive landscape, revenue model, 3-year financial projections with P&L and cash flow, marketing strategy, operations plan, and team structure. Target audience: [angel investors / VCs / bank loan officers]. Industry: [industry]. Format as a professional document with headers and data tables.
Use: Raise funding, bank loans, partnership proposals
#2
SWOT Analysis Generator
Perform a detailed SWOT analysis for [company/business idea] in the [industry] market. For each quadrant (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats), provide 5 specific points with brief explanations. Then create an action plan that leverages strengths to capture opportunities and mitigates weaknesses against threats. Include competitive context.
Use: Strategic planning, pitch decks, quarterly reviews
#3
Revenue Model Designer
Design 3 different revenue models for [business idea]. For each model: explain the pricing strategy, calculate unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback period), project monthly revenue for months 1-12, identify the break-even point, and list the key assumptions. Recommend which model to start with and why. Target market: [description].
Use: Pricing strategy, investor conversations, financial planning
#4
Competitive Analysis Matrix
Create a competitive analysis for [my product/service] against [competitor 1], [competitor 2], and [competitor 3]. Compare across these dimensions: pricing, features, target audience, strengths, weaknesses, market share, and unique value proposition. Present as a comparison matrix table. Then identify 3 gaps in the market that I can exploit.
Use: Market positioning, sales battlecards, pitch decks
#5
90-Day Launch Plan
Create a detailed 90-day launch plan for [product/service]. Break it into 3 phases (30 days each). For each phase include: specific milestones, daily/weekly tasks, marketing activities, KPIs to track, budget allocation, and potential risks with mitigation strategies. My budget is [amount]. My team size is [number].
Use: Product launches, startup kickoffs, new market entry
#6
Pitch Deck Script Writer
Write the speaking script for a 10-slide pitch deck for [startup/business]. Slides: 1) Hook/Problem, 2) Solution, 3) Market Size, 4) Product Demo Talking Points, 5) Business Model, 6) Traction/Metrics, 7) Competition, 8) Team, 9) Financials, 10) The Ask. Each slide: 3-4 sentences max. Tone: confident but not arrogant. Include suggested data points to display on each slide.
Use: Investor meetings, demo days, accelerator applications
#7
Market Sizing Calculator
Calculate the market size for [product/service] using both top-down and bottom-up approaches. Top-down: start with the total industry size and narrow to my addressable segment. Bottom-up: start with my unit economics and scale up. Provide TAM, SAM, and SOM with clear assumptions for each. Include data sources I can reference. Present as a clear breakdown with numbers.
Use: Investor pitches, business plans, go/no-go decisions
#8
Exit Strategy Planner
Outline 3 realistic exit strategies for [business type] with [current revenue] annual revenue. For each: timeline to exit, valuation method and estimated multiple, steps to prepare, key metrics acquirers care about, and potential acquirers/buyers in the market. Include what I should start doing NOW to maximize exit value in [timeframe].
Use: Long-term planning, investor conversations, board meetings
#9
Partnership Proposal Generator
Write a partnership proposal from [my company] to [target company]. Structure: 1) Why this partnership makes sense (mutual benefits), 2) Proposed collaboration model, 3) Revenue/value sharing structure, 4) Implementation timeline, 5) Success metrics, 6) Next steps. Tone: professional, specific, value-focused. Make the ROI obvious for them.
Use: Business development, co-marketing, distribution deals
#10
Business Model Canvas
Fill out a complete Business Model Canvas for [business idea]. Cover all 9 blocks: Customer Segments, Value Propositions, Channels, Customer Relationships, Revenue Streams, Key Resources, Key Activities, Key Partnerships, and Cost Structure. For each block, provide 3-5 specific items with brief explanations. Then identify the single riskiest assumption and suggest how to test it cheaply.
Use: Ideation, startup planning, pivot analysis

2. Marketing & Sales Copy (10 Prompts)

#11
High-Converting Landing Page Copy
Write landing page copy for [product/service] targeting [audience]. Include: 1) A headline that stops scrolling (use a specific number or bold claim), 2) Subheadline explaining the value in one sentence, 3) Three benefit blocks with icons, 4) Social proof section, 5) FAQ section (5 questions), 6) Two CTAs (primary and secondary). Tone: [casual/professional/urgent]. Pain point: [main problem solved].
Use: Product pages, launch pages, lead generation
#12
Email Sequence (5-Part)
Write a 5-email welcome/nurture sequence for [business]. Email 1: Welcome + quick win. Email 2: Story + credibility. Email 3: Biggest mistake + solution. Email 4: Case study/proof. Email 5: Offer + urgency. For each email: subject line (A/B variants), preview text, body (under 200 words), and CTA. Sending schedule: [daily/every 2 days/weekly].
Use: Email marketing, lead nurturing, product launches
#13
Facebook/Instagram Ad Copy (5 Variants)
Write 5 Facebook/Instagram ad copy variants for [product/service] priced at [price]. Target audience: [description]. For each variant use a different hook style: 1) Question hook, 2) Statistic hook, 3) Story hook, 4) Controversy hook, 5) FOMO hook. Each ad: primary text (under 125 chars), headline, description, and CTA button text. Optimize for [conversions/traffic/engagement].
Use: Paid social campaigns, A/B testing, retargeting
#14
Cold Email That Gets Replies
Write a cold email to [prospect type] at [company type]. Goal: [book a call / get a reply / pitch a service]. Use this structure: 1) Personalized opening (reference something specific about them), 2) One-sentence credibility, 3) The value I bring (specific to their situation), 4) Soft CTA (make it easy to say yes). Under 100 words total. Write 3 variants with different opening hooks. Also write a follow-up for non-responders (5 days later).
Use: Sales outreach, partnerships, freelance pitching
#15
Product Description (E-Commerce)
Write a product description for [product name] sold on [platform: Amazon/Shopify/Etsy]. Include: 1) SEO-optimized title (under 200 chars with keywords), 2) 5 bullet points highlighting benefits (not just features), 3) Long description (150-300 words) with emotional triggers, 4) 5 search keywords. Target buyer: [description]. Price point: [price]. Competitor products: [list].
Use: E-commerce listings, Amazon FBA, dropshipping
#16
Sales Script (Phone/Video)
Write a sales call script for selling [product/service] at [price point] to [buyer type]. Structure: 1) Opening (build rapport, 30 sec), 2) Discovery questions (5 questions that uncover pain), 3) Presentation (tie features to their specific pain), 4) Objection handling (prepare for: too expensive, need to think about it, using a competitor, bad timing), 5) Close (3 closing techniques). Total call: 20-30 minutes.
Use: Sales calls, demos, consultations
#17
LinkedIn Post That Goes Viral
Write 5 LinkedIn posts for [professional/founder in industry]. Each post should use a different viral format: 1) Contrarian take ("Everyone says X, but..."), 2) Lesson from failure, 3) Framework/list (numbered insights), 4) Before/after story, 5) Hot take on industry trend. Each post: hook in first line, 150-200 words, end with engagement question. No hashtag spam. Include line breaks for readability.
Use: Personal branding, lead generation, thought leadership
#18
Google Ads Copy Generator
Write Google Search ad copy for [product/service] targeting the keyword "[main keyword]". Create 3 ad groups, each with: 3 headlines (30 chars max each), 2 descriptions (90 chars max each), display URL path, and sitelink extensions (4). Include negative keywords to exclude. Target CPA: [amount]. Landing page: [URL]. Competitor keywords to bid on: suggest 5.
Use: PPC campaigns, search marketing, lead gen
#19
Case Study Writer
Write a customer case study for [company name] using this information: Client: [client name/type]. Challenge: [their problem]. Solution: [what we did]. Results: [metrics/outcomes]. Structure it as: 1) Executive Summary (2 sentences), 2) The Challenge, 3) The Solution (with specific steps), 4) The Results (with numbers), 5) Client Quote (write a realistic one), 6) Key Takeaways. Make it scannable with headers and bold stats.
Use: Sales collateral, website, proposals
#20
Pricing Page Copy
Write pricing page copy for [SaaS product / service] with 3 tiers. For each tier: creative name, price, tagline (who it's for), feature list (8-12 items with checkmarks), and CTA button text. Include: 1) A "Most Popular" badge on the middle tier, 2) Annual vs monthly pricing toggle text, 3) Money-back guarantee copy, 4) FAQ section (5 pricing questions), 5) Enterprise "Contact Us" CTA. Use anchoring psychology to push toward the middle tier.
Use: SaaS pricing pages, service packages, course tiers

3. Content Creation (10 Prompts)

#21
SEO Blog Article (2000 Words)
Write a 2000-word SEO-optimized blog article about "[topic]". Target keyword: "[keyword]". Include: 1) H1 title with keyword (under 60 chars), 2) Meta description (under 155 chars), 3) Introduction with hook + keyword in first 100 words, 4) H2 and H3 subheadings with related keywords, 5) Bullet points and numbered lists for scannability, 6) Internal link suggestions (3), 7) External link suggestions (2 authority sources), 8) CTA at the end. Write for [beginner/intermediate/expert] readers. Tone: [conversational/authoritative/educational].
Use: Blog strategy, organic traffic, content marketing
#22
30-Day Content Calendar
Create a 30-day social media content calendar for [business/brand] on [platforms]. For each day: 1) Content type (carousel, video, story, reel, text post), 2) Topic/hook, 3) Caption (first 2 lines), 4) Hashtags (10), 5) Best posting time. Mix content pillars: 40% value/educational, 30% engagement/community, 20% promotional, 10% personal/behind-the-scenes. Target audience: [description]. Voice: [brand voice].
Use: Social media management, brand building, agency work
#23
YouTube Video Script
Write a YouTube video script about "[topic]" for a [niche] channel. Target length: [X] minutes. Structure: 1) Hook (first 15 seconds — question, bold claim, or teaser), 2) Intro (who I am, what they'll learn, why it matters — 30 sec), 3) Main content (3-5 sections with clear transitions), 4) Recap, 5) CTA (subscribe + related video). Include [B-ROLL] markers where visual cuts would enhance engagement. Write for retention — no filler phrases.
Use: YouTube content, course creation, webinars
#24
Newsletter Issue Writer
Write a newsletter issue for [newsletter name] about [topic]. My audience is [description]. Format: 1) Subject line (3 options, under 50 chars), 2) Preview text, 3) Opening hook (personal story or surprising stat), 4) Main insight (the "aha" moment), 5) Actionable takeaway (something they can do today), 6) Resource recommendation, 7) Sign-off with personality. Under 500 words total. Make it feel like a smart friend sharing insights, not a corporate broadcast.
Use: Email newsletters, audience building, thought leadership
#25
Twitter/X Thread (10 Tweets)
Write a 10-tweet thread about [topic] for [audience]. Tweet 1: Hook that makes people stop scrolling (question, bold claim, or "I [did X]. Here's what happened:"). Tweets 2-9: One key insight per tweet, building on the previous one. Each tweet under 280 chars. Use line breaks. No hashtags in the thread body. Tweet 10: Summary + CTA (follow for more / link to resource). Make it feel like insider knowledge, not a lecture.
Use: Twitter growth, thought leadership, traffic generation
#26
Podcast Episode Outline
Create a detailed podcast episode outline about "[topic]" for [podcast name]. Target length: [X] minutes. Include: 1) Cold open hook (30 sec teaser of the best moment), 2) Intro and context, 3) Segment 1: [subtopic] with 3 talking points, 4) Segment 2: [subtopic] with 3 talking points, 5) Segment 3: listener Q&A / hot takes, 6) Key takeaways (3), 7) Sponsor read placement (mid-roll), 8) Outro with CTA. Add transition phrases between segments.
Use: Podcast planning, interview prep, content repurposing
#27
Lead Magnet Creator
Create a lead magnet about [topic] for [target audience]. Format: [checklist / cheat sheet / template / mini-guide]. Title it with a specific, benefit-driven name that makes people NEED to download it. Include: 1) Compelling title (with number if possible), 2) Subtitle explaining who it's for, 3) The actual content (10-15 actionable items), 4) Bonus tip at the end, 5) CTA to [next step: course, call, product]. Design note: suggest layout and visual hierarchy.
Use: Email list building, funnel entry point, authority building
#28
Instagram Carousel (10 Slides)
Write copy for a 10-slide Instagram carousel about "[topic]". Slide 1: Hook title (large text, curiosity-driven). Slides 2-9: One insight per slide, each with a bold header (3-5 words) and supporting text (under 30 words). Keep each slide self-contained but flowing. Slide 10: CTA (save this, follow for more, link in bio). Add design direction for each slide (background color, icon suggestion, text placement). Target: [save-worthy educational content].
Use: Instagram growth, educational content, brand authority
#29
TikTok/Reels Script Pack (5 Videos)
Write 5 TikTok/Reels scripts about [topic/niche]. Each video: 15-30 seconds. For each: 1) Hook (first 2 seconds — must stop the scroll), 2) Content (the value/entertainment), 3) CTA or punchline. Use these 5 formats: a) "3 things I wish I knew about...", b) POV story, c) Myth vs Reality, d) "Stop doing X, do Y instead", e) Quick tutorial/hack. Include on-screen text suggestions and trending sound recommendations. Optimize for shares and saves.
Use: Short-form video content, TikTok growth, viral content
#30
Webinar Presentation Script
Write a 45-minute webinar script about "[topic]" that sells [product/service] at the end. Structure: 1) Opening hook + "by the end you'll know..." promise (5 min), 2) Credibility story (3 min), 3) Content section 1: The Problem (7 min), 4) Content section 2: The Framework (10 min), 5) Content section 3: Case Studies (8 min), 6) Transition to offer (2 min), 7) The Offer (5 min), 8) Bonuses + urgency (3 min), 9) Q&A prompts (2 min). Include audience interaction points every 5-7 minutes.
Use: Selling courses, coaching, high-ticket offers
